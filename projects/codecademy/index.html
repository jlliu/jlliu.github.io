Codecademy

Jun '18 - Sep '19 · Product design

From summer of 2018 through September 2019, I worked as a product designer on Codecademy's design team. I got collaborated closely with product managers, engineers, curriculum developers and other designers to bring new features into production, all with the challenge of working within Codecademy's newly growing design system. Here are a few projects I worked on:

Onboarding

During part of my time at Codecademy, I supported designs of their new onboarding experience through its various releases.

Redesigning Onboarding

This work was meant to improve upon the company's first version of onboarding, by better orienting new learners to Codecademy's offerings as well as serving business needs of capturing data for segmentation, persona targeting and personalization.

In research done by my team prior to my design work, a main takeaway was that there were two main groups of new users - the first encompassed beginners who needed guidance, were interested in "learning to code" in general, and might not know where to begin. The second encompassed users who were more experienced, had specific outcomes, and wanted to go to their intended learning material as fast as possible.

Accordingly, I collaborated with another designer, product managers, and marketing stakeholders to mock an onboarding experience that focused on accomodating both learner types, one who needs a recommendation and one who is more self-guided. We also included a persistent option to skip onboarding altogether.

By clicking "Give me a custom recommendation.", a learner could go through a questionaire flow that simultaneously notes their interests for a personalized recommendation and helps the company better understand our learners. In the first release, by clicking "I want to explore on my own.", the learner would be taken to the course catalog.

Working through designs, I found it important to collaborate closely with marketing to ensure the copy in the questionaire was welcoming, easy to understand, and streamlined enough to avoid information overload.

I also created a navigation system that mirrored other types of navigation on the platform, to give users a sense of completion and a means to return to previous questions by clicking.

My designs also included a way to view one's catered recommendations incrementally, allowing the learner to see another one if they're curious.

Expanding on the Exploration Option

In a later release, we updated the "I want to explore on my own." option to route to a search experience in onboarding (rather than to the course catalog), to better cater to the user need of finding a specific topic or language they had in mind.

While the team had just released a general search experience for the platform, my design work incorporated this search functionality into the narrative of onboarding.

Onboarding from all Sign-up Points

On Codecademy, there are several different entry points from which a new user could sign up: the homepage, the navigation bar, the learning environment, and path (a type of learning content) landing pages. In the previous releases, only new users who signed up via the homepage were delivered onboarding.

There was a major opportunity to bring all users through onboarding, so my contribution was in thoroughly documenting all of these extraneous flows (some of which involved payment and/or took into consideration future product launches that affect signup) and proposing how onboarding might be incorporated into them.

Giving a recommendation makes sense for learners who sign up from the homepage - but how could our onboarding be framed for learners who sign up from a course or path page, i.e., those who express intent about what they learn?

While learners who want to jump into a course probably don't need a recommendation, there are still business and user benefits to capturing their interests through the questionnaire for future personalized experiences.

I designed a separate, but releated onboarding experience for these learners eager to dive into courses. This version of onboarding focuses momentum on getting the learner to get into the learning environment for their chosen subject as soon as possible, and frames the questionnaire in terms of helping personalize their Codecademy journey.

Landing Pages

I led designs on several landing pages within our platform. Each required close collaboration with members of other departments, like marketing, customer support, and our community ambassadors in order to communicate key benefits, highlight powerful testimonials, and draw attention to call-to-actions in an accessible, friendly voice.

Community @ Codecademy

In spring 2019, Codecademy had several, separate learner communities, spread across its forums, Facebook group, and Pro Community (currently hosted on Mobilize.io, and at the time on Slack).

I collaborated with a PM, another designer, and the Codecademy community manager to bring more visibility to our learner communities, guided by existing findings these spaces allow learners to help each other out, share projects and ideas, develop comraderie while going through challenging material, and later, support each other in their careers.

We landed on creating a landing page that focused on our Codecademy Pro learner community, bringing visibility to a platform otherwise only advertised to Pro members via a Customer.io notification. Codecademy for Business

Towards the end of my work with Codecademy, I also supported design for a landing page for enterprise Codecademy plans - a service that was previously handled through the customer support page.

My goal was to design a discoverable landing page that could serve as a central hub for learning about Codecademy for Business, as well as provide interested users a way to request a quote.

I also collaborated with product managers and stakeholders to mock up and discuss different potential entry points for discovering Enterprise options, eventually landing on adding a link at the bottom of the site footer as well as modifying our pricing page at the time to refer to enterprise plans.
